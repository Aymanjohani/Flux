## Daily Workflow

### Morning Routine (5 minutes)

1. **Check your notifications**
   - Stale deal alerts ‚Üí Review and update today
   - New lead assignments ‚Üí Respond within 24 hours
   - Follow-up reminders ‚Üí Complete scheduled actions

2. **Review your pipeline**
   - Sort deals by "Next Follow-up Date"
   - Handle anything due today first
   - Update stages for any progress made yesterday

3. **Log yesterday's activities**
   - If you made calls/sent emails outside HubSpot ‚Üí log them
   - Update deal notes with latest status
   - Set next follow-up dates

### Throughout the Day

- **Every contact made:** Log it in HubSpot immediately (call, email, meeting)
- **After every meeting:** Update deal stage and notes
- **When you get a response:** Update lead temperature if it changes

### End of Day (3 minutes)

- Review open tasks ‚Üí complete or reschedule
- Set follow-ups for tomorrow
- Update any deals that progressed

## Managing Contacts

### When You Get a New Lead

1. **Check if they already exist**
   - Search by email or company name first
   - Don't create duplicates

2. **Fill in required fields:**
   - ‚úÖ First Name, Last Name, Email (minimum)
   - ‚úÖ Company Name
   - ‚úÖ Phone (if you have it)
   - ‚úÖ Job Title

3. **Fill in custom fields (new!):**
   - **Manufacturing Subsector** ‚Üí What do they make?
   - **Geographic Region** ‚Üí Where are they based?
   - **Lead Source Detail** ‚Üí How did we get this lead?
   - **Lead Temperature** ‚Üí Hot/Warm/Cold (your gut feel)

4. **Assign to owner:**
   - If from your outreach ‚Üí assign to yourself
   - If inbound ‚Üí will auto-assign (workflow coming)

### Updating Contact Status

**Lifecycle Stages** (HubSpot moves these automatically, but you can override):
- **Lead** ‚Üí Just entered system, not contacted yet
- **Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL)** ‚Üí Responded to outreach
- **Sales Qualified Lead (SQL)** ‚Üí Had discovery call, good fit
- **Opportunity** ‚Üí Active deal in pipeline
- **Customer** ‚Üí Closed won
- **Evangelist** ‚Üí Happy customer, giving referrals

**When to manually update:**
- After discovery call, mark as SQL
- When deal is created, mark as Opportunity
- After project delivery, mark as Customer

### Lead Statuses (what you're actively doing)

Use these to track your current action:
- **NEW** ‚Üí Just got this lead
- **OPEN** ‚Üí Working on it
- **IN_PROGRESS** ‚Üí In active conversation
- **OPEN_DEAL** ‚Üí Deal created
- **UNQUALIFIED** ‚Üí Not a fit (wrong industry, budget, etc.)
- **ATTEMPTED_TO_CONTACT** ‚Üí Reached out, no response yet
- **CONNECTED** ‚Üí Had conversation, qualified
- **BAD_TIMING** ‚Üí Good fit, wrong time (follow up later)

## Managing Deals

### Creating a Deal

**When to create:**
- After discovery call with qualified prospect
- When they request a proposal
- When they express budget + timeline

**How to create:**
1. Go to contact record
2. Click **Create** ‚Üí **Deal**
3. Fill in:
   - **Deal Name:** `[Company] - [Solution Type]` (e.g., "ABC Food - MES Implementation")
   - **Amount:** Your estimate (update as you learn more)
   - **Close Date:** Their decision timeline
   - **Pipeline:** Keep default (you have one pipeline)
   - **Stage:** Start at first stage

4. **Fill custom fields:**
   - **Project Size Estimate** ‚Üí Small/Medium/Large/Enterprise
   - **Decision Timeline** ‚Üí Q1 2026, Q2 2026, etc.

5. **Add notes:**
   - What do they need?
   - Who are the stakeholders?
   - What's the competition?
   - Any special requirements?

### Moving Deals Through Pipeline

**Your stages (in order):**
1. **Qualification** ‚Üí Validating fit, budget, timeline
2. **Discovery/Needs Analysis** ‚Üí Understanding requirements
3. **Proposal/Quote** ‚Üí Preparing and submitting offer
4. **Negotiation** ‚Üí Discussing terms, pricing
5. **Closed Won** ‚úÖ ‚Üí Deal signed!
6. **Closed Lost** ‚ùå ‚Üí Deal fell through

**Rules:**
- Only move forward when you complete the stage's objective
- If you skip stages, that's okay (just move to current reality)
- **Always add a note** when changing stage (why did it move?)

### Deal Hygiene

**Every 7 days, update:**
- Deal stage (if changed)
- Notes (latest activity)
- Next follow-up date
- Amount (if estimate changed)

**If deal stalls:**
- Update "Next Follow-up Date" to when you'll try again
- Set reminder in calendar
- Don't leave it sitting with no plan

**If deal dies:**
- Mark as "Closed Lost"
- Add note explaining why (pricing, timing, competitor, etc.)
- Set future follow-up if appropriate (6 months, 1 year)

### Lead Temperature

**What:** How interested/ready are they to buy?

**Options:**
- **Hot** - Ready to Buy (have budget, timeline is now, high urgency)
- **Warm** - Interested (exploring options, timeline 3-6 months)
- **Cold** - Future Opportunity (awareness stage, 6+ months out)

**Why it matters:**
- Prioritizes your outreach (hot leads first)
- Determines follow-up frequency
- Used for lead scoring

**When to update:**
- Initial: Your gut feel from first research
- After discovery call: Based on budget, timeline, urgency
- Ongoing: As their situation changes

**Examples:**
- Hot: "We need MES by Q2, budget approved, RFP out"
- Warm: "Interested in digitalizing, evaluating vendors"
- Cold: "Just learning about Industry 4.0, no timeline"

### IIoT Lead Score (0-100)

**What:** Automated score based on engagement + fit

**How it works:**
- System calculates automatically (workflow coming)
- Based on: emails opened, website visits, downloads, company size, industry

**What you do:**
- **Nothing!** This is automatic
- Just check it to prioritize outreach (higher score = better lead)

**Score guide:**
- 80-100: Hot, prioritize immediately
- 60-79: Warm, reach out this week
- 40-59: Warm, reach out this month
- 0-39: Cold, nurture campaign

### Competitive Status

**What:** Do they have an existing system? Who's the incumbent?

**Options:**
- No Incumbent System (greenfield - best scenario!)
- Siemens Installed
- ABB Installed
- Schneider Installed
- Rockwell Installed
- Other Vendor
- Unknown

**Why it matters:**
- Shapes your pitch (integration vs. replacement)
- Informs pricing strategy
- Sets expectations (brownfield is complex)

**When to fill:** After discovery call

**What to do with it:**
- Greenfield ‚Üí Emphasize speed, best practices
- Siemens/ABB/Schneider ‚Üí Emphasize interoperability, vendor-agnostic approach
- Unknown ‚Üí Ask during discovery

---

### Last Contact Attempt Date

**What:** When did you last try to reach this person?

**Why it matters:**
- Prevents over-contacting (don't spam)
- Tracks follow-up cadence
- Used for re-engagement campaigns

**When to fill:**
- Every time you call, email, or message
- Even if they don't respond

**Auto-updates when:**
- You send email from HubSpot
- You log a call in HubSpot

---

### Next Follow-up Date

**What:** When should you contact them again?

**Why it matters:**
- Creates your daily task list
- Prevents leads from going cold
- Workflow will remind you when date arrives

**When to set:**
- After every interaction
- Based on their timeline/interest

**Guidelines:**
- Hot leads: 2-3 days
- Warm leads: 1 week
- Cold leads: 1 month
- Post-proposal: 3-5 days

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Inbound Lead from Website

**Steps:**
1. Check if contact exists ‚Üí search by email
2. If new ‚Üí create contact
3. Fill in: Name, Email, Company, Phone (if provided)
4. Set custom fields:
   - Lead Source Detail: "Website Form"
   - Lead Temperature: "Warm" (they reached out!)
   - Geographic Region: Check company address
   - Manufacturing Subsector: Research or ask
5. Assign to yourself (or leave for auto-assignment)
6. Respond within 24 hours
7. Set "Next Follow-up Date" for 2-3 days

### Scenario 2: Cold Outreach via LinkedIn

**Steps:**
1. Research company first (what they make, size, location)
2. Create contact in HubSpot before reaching out
3. Fill custom fields:
   - Lead Source Detail: "LinkedIn Outreach"
   - Lead Temperature: "Cold"
   - Manufacturing Subsector: [from research]
   - Geographic Region: [from research]
4. Send connection request + message
5. Log activity: "LinkedIn connection request sent"
6. Set "Last Contact Attempt Date": Today
7. Set "Next Follow-up Date": 7 days (if no response)

### Scenario 3: Discovery Call Went Well

**Steps:**
1. Update contact:
   - Lifecycle Stage: "Sales Qualified Lead"
   - Lead Status: "CONNECTED"
   - Lead Temperature: "Warm" or "Hot" (based on urgency)
2. Fill in custom fields you learned:
   - Competitive Status: [what system they have now]
   - Manufacturing Subsector: [confirmed]
3. Create deal:
   - Deal Name: "[Company] - [Solution]"
   - Amount: Your estimate
   - Close Date: Their timeline
   - Stage: "Discovery/Needs Analysis"
   - Project Size Estimate: [Small/Medium/Large/Enterprise]
   - Decision Timeline: [Q1/Q2/etc]
4. Add detailed notes:
   - Pain points
   - Stakeholders
   - Budget range
   - Timeline
   - Competition
5. Set "Next Follow-up Date": Based on their timeline
6. Create tasks:
   - Prepare proposal
   - Send case studies
   - Schedule demo

### Scenario 4: Deal Went Cold

**Steps:**
1. Don't just leave it hanging!
2. Move to "Closed Lost"
3. Add note explaining:
   - Why: No budget / Bad timing / Chose competitor / No response
   - Details: What happened
4. If it's timing-based:
   - Set "Next Follow-up Date" for future (6 months, 1 year)
   - Add note: "Follow up in Q3 2026 when budget renews"
5. Update "Lead Temperature" to "Cold"
6. Keep contact in system (don't delete!)

### Scenario 5: Customer Wants to Expand

**Steps:**
1. They're already a customer (Lifecycle: Customer)
2. Create new deal:
   - Deal Name: "[Company] - [New Project]"
   - Amount: Estimate
   - Stage: "Discovery/Needs Analysis" (or wherever appropriate)
3. Add note: "Expansion from existing customer"
4. Link to original deal:
   - Go to Associations
   - Add related deal
5. Easier sales process (existing relationship!)
